Background

Radia Perlman is an underrepresented innovator in the world of IT, Computer Science and Networking.

Radia was born on December 18, 1951 in Portsmouth, Virginia. Perlman earned her PhD at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1988. Perlman is also known for being gifted in music and poetry. She is often referred to as the "Mother of the Internet," a title which she humbly denies, citing that the internet only is what it is today due to the contributions of numerous bright minds in her field. She wrote a wonderfully illustrative poem, or as she would call it, her algorithm's "Algorhyme" about her most well-known contribution, Spanning-Tree Protocol, or STP, and we really wanted to highlight it here.

Algorhyme


I think that I shall never see
A graph more lovely than a tree.
A tree whose crucial property
Is loop-free connectivity.
A tree which must be sure to span
So packets can reach every LAN.
First the root must be selected.
By ID it is elected.
Least cost paths from root are traced.
In the tree these paths are placed.
A mesh is made by folks like me
Then bridges find a spanning tree.

-Radia Perlman

Contributions to Technology

Before delving into the obligatory list of Radia Perlman's contributions to technology, informatics, and computing, we wanted to highlight something about her that we've already briefly touched on, something that her colleagues deem an integral element of her contributions to any field. To quote the article Radia Perlman on She Thought It, "Radia Perlman is not only known for her major contributions to the Internet, but also for being a poet and for loving music," they go on to quote Perlman as insisting, "Every algorithm ought to have an associated algorhyme" (Stamatopoulou, 2019). In a field of technologically fascinating, yet dour and rigid whitepapers and other scholarly literature, we posit that Radia Perlman brought a warm, human element to a somewhat cold, mechanical field. Her radiant enthusiasm for technology, networking, and telecommunications alone is worth highlighting. Again, to quote She Thought It, "she didn't hesitate to play on the piano the piece Rondo Capriccioso, composed by the French composer Camille Saint-Saëns, for a National Inventors Hall of Fame video, or to perform stand-up comedy in a passenger talent show, while she was on a cruise to Russia to see a solar eclipse" (Stamatopoulou, 2019). The many facets to her personality are truly inspiring and, innovations aside, if more people were like Radia Perlman, the world would be a much more jovial place.

Radia Perlman approached technology with a zeal commensurate with her enthusiasm for life. To say that she found equal success would be an understatement. She worked hand in hand with International Business Machines (IBM) to develop several routing standards, predominantly the Open Systems Interconnections Interdomain Routing Protocol (IDRP) (Stamatopoulou, 2019). In addition to IBM, she also worked with Cisco Systems Inc. to standardize the IDRP protocol by serving on the prestigious American National Standards Institute (ANSI) sub-committee (Stamatopoulou, 2019). Aside from her contributions to IDRP, Perlman also played such a significant role in early innovation of Interconnected Networks and the Internet that she was dubbed the, "Mother of the Internet" (LMIT, 2023). This moniker was earned primarily due to her invention of the algorithm behind the aforementioned Spanning Tree Protocol (STP), which was a critical piece of the puzzle of high-density, broad-spectrum information routing (LMIT, 2023). To quote LMIT, Perlman is described as, "having put the 'basic traffic rules' into place for the Internet" (LMIT, 2023). This protocol remains in use to this day, having seen widespread adoption by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) as a standard for bridge technology (LMIT, 2023). While Perlman contributed so much to the fields of information technology, telecommunications, and computer science as a whole, her last innovation that we wanted to highlight was to the field of network security. While working for Sun Microsystems, she reconstituted her previous breakthroughs with STP but on a cybersecurity front when she redesigned her algorithm to be "more robust, more stable, and... to establish optimal paths between points" while working on a project dubbed Transparent Interconnection of Lots of Links, or TRILL (LMIT, 2023). In addition to TRILL, she also pushed cryptography forward with her "ephemerizer" project, which enabled users to ensure that encrypted data couldn't be accessed after a preset expiration date, via subsequent destruction of encryption keys (LMIT, 2023). Radia Perlman made so many innovations to her many fields, and aside from her clever naming conventions, ingenuity, and creativity, she was always the light in an otherwise dark room, and her poetry makes understanding some of the most complex issues in telecommunications trivial and a joy.
