Digital Campaign Process

Prior to creating our group's Digital Artifacts to promote Radia Perlman, we learned how to use Adobe XD to create prototype social media posts (Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter). We compiled our Individual Digital Artifacts into a single document and have linked them below (we highlighted Clarence “Skip” Ellis in our individual artifacts, another underrepresented innovator in technology).
Individual Digital Artifacts

The first thing that we did as a group was to create Digital Artifacts to represent Radia Perlman, an underrepresented innovator in technology that we chose to highlight with our group's Digital Campaign. We created prototype posts with outlines from Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter. A link to view our group's Digital Artifacts is below.
Group Digital Artifacts

The second step for our group was to create a Website Wireframe for our project. Our Website Wireframe provides information about Radia Perlman and information about us, the creators of this website, and the minds behind our Digital Campaign to highlight Radia Perlman. We also used this wireframe as a prototype for this website, and you'll notice the design of this website is very similar to our Website Wireframe linked below.
Website Wireframe

Our third step as a group was to create a Survey to get feedback on our group's original Digital Artifacts. Our Survey asks typical survey questions, and more questions that are specific to our group's Digital Artifacts promoting Radia Perlman. You can take a look at our Survey at the link below (you can also take our original Survey at the link below too).
Original Survey

The fourth step our group took was to send out our survey for consideration. We sent the survey to as many people as possible and recorded their responses in a Microsoft Excel spreadsheet. We also cleaned the respondents' data and created some minor data visualizations in our Excel spreadsheet. You can find our Cleaned Survey Data at the link below.
Survey Data (Cleaned)

Our group did further exercises in data visualization using Google's Looker Studio. We created some simple Data Visualizations based on PEW Research Survey Data as well as a simple Digital Dashboard containing more data visualizations composed in a compelling and accessible manner. To view our PEW Data Visualizations, click the link below.
PEW Data Visualization

Before our Digital Campaign highlighting Radia Perlman went live, we took the skills that we learned throughout this course and put them to use creating Data Visualizations in both Microsoft Excel and Google's Looker Studio. We created a Pivot Table in Excel and another Digital Dashboard in Looker Studio using our initial Survey response data. We linked the Excel file for our Survey Data Visualization, our Google Looker Studio Survey Data Digital Dashboard, and PDF Documentation containing explanations on how and why we created the Data Visualizations that we did below.

Our final step before launching our Digital Campaign was to present our process and various deliverables (many of which are included on this page) to our I101: Introduction to Informatics class. We learned a lot throughout this course, and this Digital Campaign project was a culmination of those skills, as well as an opportunity to work together as a group and get to know our classmates. We wrapped up our semester together by creating a Microsoft PowerPoint Presentation and presenting it to our classmates during the last week of labs for this course. We converted our Group Presentation to a PDF document and have linked it below.
Group Presentation

Finally, we officially launched our Digital Campaign for Radia Perlman (which consisted of three main steps)! The first of these steps was to update our Survey based on feedback that we received from our Original Survey in Step 3. We appreciated the time that everyone spent responding to our original Survey, so we considered their feedback and created an Updated Survey to gather feedback on our Digital Campaign after it went live. Our Updated Survey can be found at the link below (please take the time to take our Updated Survey at this link too, we really appreciate your time and feedback)!
Updated Survey

Our next step in the process of launching our Digital Campaign was to update our Digital Artifacts based on feedback we received in our initial Survey and begin posting them to our social media pages. You can find our Final Digital Artifacts compiled into a PDF document at the link below (and if you know us in real life, you might just see them in our social media feeds in the coming days).
Final Digital Artifacts

Finally, we were ready to launch our Website. If you're reading this, you obviously made it to our website! Please, take a moment to give us feedback on our Updated Survey as we'd greatly appreciate it! While we were working on the website we used GitHub to more easily maintain and collaborate on our Website code. We also took advantage of the GitHub pages feature to host our final site on GitHub as well. Another feature that we spent a lot of time working on and perfecting was our Website's responsiveness, which you'll notice if you browse our website from a cellphone or mobile browser! We wanted to make sure that our website was accessible from computers and cell phones. Our GitHub Repository, where all of our code is located and publicly available, is linked below, as well as our GitHub Pages site (which is an alternate host for our Website).
